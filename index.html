How Well do You
Know Whit's Website?
We tested one dig-245: Critical Web Design student in order to find out.
Flavor of the Week

Thatcher found the flavor of the week in 12 seconds. He used his context clues and past website experience when he chose to go directly to the top website menu to look for the flavor of the week schedule!

Gluten Free Options

Thatcher was already very familiar with the Whit’s website before this task and took only 9 seconds to locate a gluten-free menu option. Thatcher used logical deduction to determine that gluten-free options would be under the “menu” tab of the top website menu.

Whit's To-Go

Thatcher found Whit’s to-go fairly quickly (in 30 seconds) because there was a shortcut on the page he was on. However, a user would normally have to go to the bottom of the website to place an order.

Whit's in Mount Airy

Finding a specific Whit’s location took Thatcher the longest at 1 minute and 30 seconds. When he went to “Stores Coming Soon” and didn’t find current locations, it took him a minute to find the “Our Stores” tab in the main menu. If I were the website designer, I would have put current and coming locations together.

Whit's on Twitter

Thatcher found the Whit’s Twitter handle in 11 seconds, using past website experience to locate it on the bottom of the page.

Conclusion

Some small navigational nuances aside, Whit’s has a clean, easy-to-use website designed for the ultimate user experience.
